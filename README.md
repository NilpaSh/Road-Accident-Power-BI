# Road-Accident-Power-BI

Technical Test - BI Specialist
Instructions:
•	The test is designed to evaluate skills in data modeling, KPI creation, and report generation.
•	Use tools you consider necessary. Power BI is required for visualization.
•	Submit your solution as a PBIX file, along with the source files.
•	You can add any information you consider relevant to improve the analysis.
Scenario:
Road Accident Analysis
The company specializing in logistics solutions, MasterLogistics, is one of the largest companies in the sector in Brazil and is currently seeking to expand its area of operation to the northern region of South America.
Senior management has requested the security team to conduct a study of the main road transport routes in the region. The initial investigation concluded that the areas with the highest concentration of accidents are the main roads in the country.
Based on this initial assessment, the company determined that the Analytics department should develop a dashboard to deepen the analysis. You, as members of the team, received the following email from the company's management:
Good morning,
For this expansion project, we need a detailed study on the records of accidents on federal highways in Brazil. It will be necessary to develop the dashboard containing information corresponding to the years 2018, 2019, and 2020.
In a meeting with the company's strategic area, we identified the following essential points:
•	We need to quantify the total number of events that occurred, with views by hours, day of the week, and season of the year.
•	It is also required to collect the number of fatalities and injuries.
•	A map with the geolocation of the events must be presented.
•	It is mandatory to evaluate the "Top 7" causes of accidents.
•	Do not forget to quantify the occasions when a single accident recorded more than 3 victims.
•	It is extremely important that all information is included on a single page of the dashboard and that it is also possible to filter by months, years, and the classification defined for each incident.
Feel free to propose additional analyses if you wish on new pages.
